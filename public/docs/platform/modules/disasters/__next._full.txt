8:T12af,# Disaster & Incident Management

INMACOM MIS provides a coordinated transboundary dashboard for logging and managing water-related disaster incidents (e.g. chemical spills, floods, dam failures) and routing alerts to downstream countries.

---

## The Incident Workflow

```
   ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │        Clerk/Manager reports Incident locally          │
   └───────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────┘
                               ▼
   ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ System generates a unique incident Reference number    │
   └───────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────┘
                               ▼
   ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ Renders coordinates on Map; Flags downstream warning   │
   └───────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────┘
                               ▼
   ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ Transboundary Notifications dispatched (SMS/Email)     │
   └───────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────┘
                               ▼
   ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ Responders document Actions (Mitigation & Recovery)   │
   └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## Database Schema & Details

Incidents are stored in `disaster_incidents` and linked to auxiliary tables.

### 1. `disaster_incidents`
Stores core incident metadata:
*   `reference`: Auto-generated unique string (e.g. `INC-2026-0034`).
*   `hazard_code`: Type of hazard (e.g., `flood`, `chemical_spill`, `dam_discharge`).
*   `incident_status`: `reported`, `active`, `mitigated`, or `resolved`.
*   `severity_level`: `minor`, `moderate`, `major`, or `critical`.
*   `latitude` & `longitude`: Geolocation of the incident source.

### 2. `pollution_incident_details`
When the hazard code is a chemical spill or pollution event, this table stores EPA-specific metrics:
*   `usepa_category`: USEPA classification index (1-4).
*   `pollutant_name` & `cas_number`: Chemical identifiers.
*   `spill_volume_m3` & `estimated_mass_kg`: Physical scale of the spill.
*   `fish_kill_observed` & `fish_kill_extent`: Ecological impact flags.
*   `waterborne_disease_reported` & `disease_name`: Public health threats.

---

## Transboundary Notification Routing

One of the most critical aspects of the treaty is the requirement to alert neighboring countries of upstream pollution or flood events.
*   **Table:** `incident_notifications`
*   **Routing Fields:**
    *   `recipient_country`: The target country (e.g. `MZ` for Mozambique when a spill occurs in `ZA` South Africa).
    *   `recipient_org`: The target department (e.g., *ARA-Sul* in Mozambique).
    *   `channel`: `email`, `sms`, or `internal_alert`.
    *   `acknowledged_at` & `acknowledged_by`: Tracks when the downstream authority received and verified the notification.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The system requires that any incident flagged as `major` or `critical` severity at a border catchment automatically dispatches a notification record to the adjacent country. This ensures compliance with the IIMA Article 12 (Emergency Situations).

---

## Response Actions Logging

All mitigation and recovery operations are tracked in the `incident_actions` table:
*   `phase`: `preparation`, `containment`, `mitigation`, or `recovery`.
*   `action_type`: Type of action taken (e.g. closing downstream water intake valves).
