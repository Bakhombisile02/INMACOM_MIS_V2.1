5:T754,# Compliance & Disasters

This section covers treaty compliance monitoring, threshold configuration, and disaster hazard reporting.

---

## 1. Compliance Thresholds (IIMA)

To monitor and configure water treaty limits, managers navigate to the **Compliance Thresholds** tab.

![Compliance Thresholds](/screenshots/thresholds.png)

From this interface, Water Resource Managers can:
- **Instream Flow Requirements (Eflows):** Configure the minimum flow rate required at cross-border stations (e.g. South Africa/Mozambique border). If a daily average drops below this value, a critical alert is triggered.
- **Water Quality Limits:** Establish target ranges for parameters like salinity, pH, or EC. Exceeding these limits highlights the station in yellow on the main map.
- **Water Allocations:** Set maximum annual abstraction limits for each country and monitoring sector (irrigation, municipal, industrial).

---

## 2. Disaster incident Alerting

In the event of floods, dam spillages, or chemical spills, users must quickly report a **Disaster Incident**:

1. Navigate to the **Disaster Management** tab.
2. Click **Report Incident**.
3. Select the affected **Management Area** and fill in the details:
   - **Type:** Flood, pollution, dam failure, etc.
   - **Severity:** Low, Medium, High, Critical.
   - **Description:** Summary of the hazard.
4. Click **Submit**.

---

## 3. Downstream Notifications

- **Alert Generation:** An immediate notification is sent to all registered users of downstream countries. For example, if a chemical spill is reported in South Africa or Eswatini, a critical alert email and SMS is dispatched to ARA-Sul managers in Mozambique.
