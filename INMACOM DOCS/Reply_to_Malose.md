## 1. Ministers' meeting — what will we present, and in what format?

The Council of Ministers meets on 4 and 5 June 2026. That gives us a firm and short window. Our recommendation is that we present a **working, navigable demonstration of the IMS** — not a fully commissioned V2 deployment, but a platform that shows the Commission is managing its information seriously and collaboratively. Concretely, the ministers' audience is most interested in seeing:

- that monitoring stations across the three countries are visible on a shared platform;
- that flows, dam levels and water-quality data (where available) can be read by any Party;
- that compliance with IIMA minimum-flow obligations is checked automatically, not assembled by hand; and
- that the platform is accessible and professional.

The format of the work between now and the meeting follows a straightforward progression:

1. **Fix the immediate issues** (the numbered list 1.23–1.35 in your document) — this is the current active task list;
2. **Present and review** — a working session with INMACOM and the VUNWE hackathon partners before the ministers' meeting so that all nominated staff have seen the platform in operation;
3. **Polish** — final refinements and testing; and
4. **Hand over the acceptance package** — the nine criteria in §9.2 of the *DDR* become the reference document for INMACOM's ongoing health check of the platform.

## 5. Risks of proceeding before commissioning — what do we present to commissioners?

V2 is the platform we are taking to the ministers' meeting. It has the capability, the architecture and the treaty-anchored foundation that the current platform does not. The approach is therefore to **demonstrate and test V2 during the joint working week**, resolve any issues as they arise, and arrive at the ministers' meeting with a system that has been exercised under real conditions.

What this means in practice:

- The working week (starting Monday) is both the data-upload week and the active testing period. Issues found are fixed in the same window.
- What we present to the ministers is a working V2 demonstration — stations, data, compliance dashboard, document library — against live data loaded that week.
- What we present to the commissioners is the nine §9.2 acceptance-criteria checklist, run in their presence, together with the operator hand-over documentation. That formal commissioning review follows the ministers' meeting on a schedule to be agreed.

## 6. The eight-module database — is this the new approach?

It is the revitalised approach, not an entirely new one. The eight-module structure — Users & Access, Monitoring Stations, Measurements & Verification, Management Areas, IIMA Compliance, Disaster Management, Documents, Audit — builds directly on the architectural thinking in the original platform. What is new in V2 is that the modules are fully treaty-anchored (all IIMA subcatchments, IAAP-10 water-use allocations, 19 ecological-flow key points, 37 REIWQ Appendix A parameters, IAAP-5 hazard tables) and the verification gate is operationally enforced. The foundation is the same house, properly furnished and with its locks changed.

## 9. Section 4.5 and knowledge transfer

To clarify the structure of the proposal:

- **Section 4.3 — Knowledge transfer, built to resist the silo** is the direct answer to your question about the development team. It covers: operator hand-over documentation (English, with Portuguese and siSwati summaries), a working session with INMACOM and the VUNWE hackathon partners covering the verification workflow and core modules, and the documented §9.2 acceptance criteria that INMACOM can run as an annual health check without Datamatics being present.

- **Section 4.5 — The durable returns** is not about the handover process. It describes what the Commission gets in the long run: defensible bilateral data, treaty compliance computed by the system rather than assembled by hand, and a modular architecture that VUNWE and hackathon partners can extend without re-engineering.

For the development team specifically, the plan is: online documentation, structured training sessions, and standing online follow-up for questions and requests after handover. This is designed so that the knowledge does not concentrate in any single person — which is precisely the failure mode that left the original platform underutilised.
